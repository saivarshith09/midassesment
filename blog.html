A TRIP TO LEH LADHAK

JULLEY

About

The Ladakh capital city of Leh lies near the eastern parts of Jammu and Kashmir, on the crossroads of the historic "Silk Route" from Sinkiang to West Asia and to the plains of India. The humbling monasteries of Shey, Hemis, Alchi, Thikse and Lamayuru will nurture your spiritual needs, and the landscape of Leh provides for a number of adventure activities including mountaineering, white-water rafting and trekking along the Markha Valley.

Leh Ladakh is a breathtaking destination renowned for its serene landscapes and vibrant culture. With rivers like the Indus, Zanskar, and Shyok flowing against the backdrop of the Himalayas, it offers mesmerizing views and tranquility. Must-visit attractions include the famous Khardung La Pass, the gravity-defying Magnetic Hill, and the serene Shanti Stupa. Adventure enthusiasts can indulge in trekking, camping, river rafting, and backpacking trips, while food lovers can savor local delicacies like thukpa and butter tea. Shopping for souvenirs and handicrafts in the local markets adds to the experience of exploring the Land of Lamas.

Pangong lake

Pangong Lake, nestled at 14,270 feet altitude, enchants travelers with its stunning beauty and unique features. Stretching 12 kilometers along the India-China border, its color-changing waters shift from blue to red and green hues. Despite its salty nature, the lake freezes in winter, creating a mesmerizing sight of thick ice sheets. Pangong's pristine environment attracts migratory birds, delighting birdwatchers. Camping by the lakeside and enjoying bonfires under the starlit sky add to the allure, making it a must-visit for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

KHARDUNG LA Overview

Magnetic Hill in Ladakh, located on the Leh-Kargil-Baltic National Highway, is renowned for its mysterious gravitational anomaly. Vehicles parked at a specific marked spot on this hill appear to move uphill on their own, defying gravity. Despite various scientific theories attempting to explain the phenomenon, such as optical illusions or the Earth's magnetic force, the allure of Magnetic Hill remains a captivating experience for tourists worldwide. Whether it's the belief in local superstitions or the fascination with natural wonders, Magnetic Hill continues to attract visitors seeking to witness this intriguing phenomenon and enjoy the scenic beauty of Ladakh.

NUBRA VALLEY Overview

Pass, at 5,359 meters elevation, is the world's highest motorable pass and a gateway to Ladakh's stunning Shyok and Nubra Valleys. Offering breathtaking vistas and steeped in historical significance as a vital link on the ancient caravan route to Central Asia, it served as a crucial conduit for supplies during World War II. Maintained by the Border Roads Organisation since its construction in 1976, it remains integral for transporting goods to the Siachen Glacier. A must-visit for adventurers and tourists, it promises a blend of adventure, history, and unparalleled natural beauty.

Tso Moriri Lake Overview

Tso Moriri Lake, nestled in the serene Rupshu Valley of Leh Ladakh, offers a heavenly retreat for travelers. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains and stunning landscapes, this lake sits at an altitude of approximately 4,000 meters, spanning 29 km in length and 8 km in width. It's a haven for nature enthusiasts, bird watchers, and photographers, boasting diverse species of exotic birds, both native and migratory. As the largest high altitude lake in India, Tso Moriri falls under the Tsomoriri Wetland Conservation Reserve area. To experience its breathtaking beauty, it's best to visit during the summer months and obtain the mandatory Inner Line Permit.

Diskit Gompa Overview

Diskit Gompa, situated at an altitude of 3,144 meters in the Nubra Valley of Leh, is the largest monastery in the region. Belonging to the Gelugpa sect of Tibetan Buddhism, it offers serene surroundings with breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks and the Shyok River flood plains. The monastery is renowned for its historical and mythological significance, featuring a majestic 100 ft statue of Maitreya Buddha and stunning paintings of Buddhist deities. Its grand architecture and serene ambiance make it a must-visit destination in Ladakh.

Shanti Stupa Overview

Shanti Stupa, located atop a hill in Chanspa, Leh Ladakh, offers breathtaking views and serenity. Built by Japanese monks to promote world peace and commemorate 2500 years of Buddhism, this white beauty stands at 4267 meters altitude amidst snow-capped mountains. Just 5 km from Leh City, it provides a peaceful retreat from the noise, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking tranquility and exotic vistas.

Kargil Overview

Kargil, the second-largest city in Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir, boasts rich cultural and historical significance. Situated at 2676 meters above sea level along the Suru or Indus River, it experiences extreme temperatures, dropping to -45 degrees Celsius in winters. Once predominantly Tibetan-Buddhist, the population converted to Islam in the 18th and 19th centuries, with most being of mixed Dard and Tibetan descent. Known for the Kargil War of 1999, it has since become a peaceful and stunning tourist destination, nestled amidst the majestic Himalayan mountains and the pristine waters of the Indus River.

Royal Leh Palace Overview

The Royal Leh Palace, dating back to the 16th century, is a magnificent attraction in Leh Ladakh, offering panoramic views of the town and Zanskar valley. Standing nine stories tall, it once housed royal families on the upper floors and stables and storerooms on the lower levels. Visitors can admire ancient Chinese paintings dating back 450 years and soak in the beauty of the historic era.
